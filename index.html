Deep learning advances: unsupervised pretraining

These series intend to shed some light on the problems and corresponding solutions that have followed the development of deep learning. I don’t intend to make this a comprehensive review of all techniques but to select some of the prominent ones. In the early development of neural networks, the initialization of parameters was performed by sampling a uniform distribution bounded to a predetermined interval. In this process, the weights and biases produced solutions that would generally lie far from an optimum set of parameters, requiring many training iterations to readjust.
